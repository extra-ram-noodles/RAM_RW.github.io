Slug cat pollo

Scugpollos predominately resemble slugcats as they're closely related, however they are much more short and stout, and lack the dexterous-ish hands of a scug, furthermore they posses a much rounder build, their fore (the section which stands erect)
Unlike scugs, scugpollos are not hermaphroditic and so are separated into males and females, the difference is quite easy to tell in mature individuals, as females are larger than males and posses a mane-like formation of fat.
Wild scugpollos are already quite the pudgy creatures, and after domestication for their eggs and meat, they became even more portly, however they were not subjected to the same artificial alterations as chickens (not in any large scale anyway), as the ancients learned more chicken in every chicken makes the chicken not taste chicken-y enough.
Withal some ancients preferred eating wild scugpollos as they were superior in taste. And they weren't particularly hard game at any rate.
